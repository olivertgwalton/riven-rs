# Usenet

Direct-streaming Usenet downloader. Rather than downloading an NZB to disk and unpacking
it, this plugin parses the NZB, stores its segment metadata, and hands the VFS a
`usenet://` URL — bytes are pulled from NNTP on demand as the player seeks. Nothing is
staged locally.

Pair it with [newznab](newznab.md), which does the actual searching.

## Providers

`nntpproviders` holds one entry per account. Servers marked `backup` are only tried once
every primary has failed, which is the right setting for block accounts. Within the
primaries, lower `priority` is tried first. Keep `max_connections` at or below what your
provider allows — exceeding it gets connections refused, not throttled.

## Verifying releases before committing

Usenet articles expire, and a release that looks complete in the indexer may be missing
articles. The verification settings trade grab speed against the risk of a title stalling
mid-playback:

- `availabilitysamplepercent` (default 5) spot-checks that share of a release. Cheap, but
  sampling can miss a single bad file.
- `checkallsegments` checks every file. Slower, and the only reliable way to catch one
  missing file. Turn this on if titles keep stalling.
- `verifypar2blocks` checks RAR volumes against PAR2 checksums, catching a volume with
  the *wrong* content rather than a missing one. Unlike the others it downloads real data,
  which is why it is off by default.
- `acceptablemissingpercent` should stay at 0 — one missing file is enough to stall playback.

`autorepair` re-downloads titles the background health scanner finds broken, backing off
between attempts (1h, 2h, 4h…) up to `repairmaxretries`. `blacklistonreadfailure` reacts
at playback time instead: the moment a read hits a missing file, the release is blacklisted
and swapped for another.
